A Company's Novel Website Design in Adobe XD

SDRC's online journey started with a brainstorming session on a piece of paper in a lounge somewhere in The Bahamas. Because SDRC had always found clients through referrals, the company's founder waited years to establish a web presence. When the time came, she knew she wanted something with bright, cheerful colors and simple language that would be easy for her international users to understand (even without the embedded translation button).



She wanted to communicate her company's mission and scientific methods without boring the user (or giving away her firm's secret recipe). Developing user identities turned out to be key in targeting her message.

After a paper wireframe sketch, I used Sketch software to make a prototype of the website. I tested the prototype with people inside and outside the SCUBA and resort industry (and it tested poorly). Based on feedback, we revisited our images and some of the content to make the company's services and value proposition more clear.

Even the most savvy designers need input from users to deliver a successful product. Knowing your audience is so important in user experience design because they surprise you! Our user feedback indicated that the underwater scuba pictures were pretty but made the company's product offering confusing. We also lacked a clear "call to action" on each page. Learning these lessons early in the development process made adapting the product easy--and saved a lot of money on development costs.
